Concept and Development

Cockatoo Expression Sheet

This piece, while not the first, was one of the few beginner drawings I completed when learning Krita. I had wanted extra practice using the application so I was sketching out a bunch of ideas of what I could draw. The concept for the piece began with this small doodle in the corner of my brainstorming page. I decided to develop the concept more as I liked the idea of using a bird companion that was not the more 'classical' witch comapanion of raven, crow, or owl.

My initial digital sketch(s) then looked like this as I pretty early on chose to make the layout of the whole piece more as an character/expression sheet for the bird, with just the added pose with the witch on the end, so that more of the canvas could be utilized. Afterwords I decided on a color scheme and started blocking out the colors. Over time as I was drawing changed the pose and outfit of the witch to be less fierce/sharp and instead make her appear more friendly to match with the cockatoo (as they are considered highly social and expressive birds.)

Throughout the process I learned a lot, by watching more tutorials on the features in Krita and through simple trial and error until I made it work. In the end it was a fun experience to practice using a the newer application, as well as practice in drawing an person, which is a subject I have wanted to practice doing more.
