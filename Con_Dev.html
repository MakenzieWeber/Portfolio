Concept and Development

Cockatoo Expression Sheet

This piece, while not the first, was one of the few beginner drawings I completed when learning Krita. I had wanted extra practice using the application so I was sketching out a bunch of ideas of what I could draw. The concept began with this small doodle in the corner of my brainstorming page. I decided to develop the concept more as I liked the idea of using a bird companion that was not the more 'classical' witch comapanion of raven, crow, or owl.

My initial digital sketch(s) then looked like this as I pretty early on chose to make the layout of the whole piece more as an character/expression sheet for the bird, with just the added pose with the witch on the end, so that more of the canvas could be utilized. I was inspired a bit by hawk/falcon trainers which is why I gave the witch longer sleeves and gloves, and then also decided to base my bird off of a sulphur-crested cockatoo as that was what parrot I thought best matched the pencil sketch. After the digital sketches were done I decided on a color scheme and started blocking out where I wanted the colors to go. Over time as I was drawing I altered the pose and outfit of the witch to be less fierce/sharp and more friendly to match with the cockatoo (as they are considered highly social and expressive birds.)
As I was still learning how best to color and shade using the application and I learned only after I completed the entire artwork that I could isolate layers to more easily shade them, so instead I had made seperate layers for each piece for shading and then separated groups of those pieces into groups, and then made more groups out of those. Which ended up resulting in lots of lagging and more difficulty in drawing. So out of everything I learned though this experince, one lesson I know will stick best with me is to merge groups when I am happy with them, and to avoid making seperate shading layers when possible.

Throughout the process I learned a lot, by watching more tutorials on the features in Krita and through simple trial and error until I made it work. In the end it was a fun experience to practice using a the newer application, as well as practice in drawing an person, which is a subject I have wanted to practice doing more.
